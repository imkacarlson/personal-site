Willamette University

Fall 2015

Monday, Wednesday, Friday 8:00 - 9:00 AM
I took two years of Spanish in high school but that was freshman and sophomore year so I do not think I could have tested out of any of the language requirements. I decided to take German just because it seemed new and fun. It ended up going well I think the coolest part was learning a lot about German culture.
Monday, Wednesday, Friday 9:10 - 10:10 AM
I decided to take this math class because it is required for the Computer Science major. I am certain I want to major in computer science and I want to get the math out of the way ASAP so why not start now. The toughest part of this class was that it was Accelerated so it was basically two classes in one semester. When the normal midterms were going on we were having out final for 152 and a quarter of the way into the semester we had our midterm. It was a big change from High School but I enjoyed the challenge.
Monday, Wednesday, Friday 10:20 - 11:20 AM (Lecture), 1:50 - 2:50 PM (Lab)
I want to major in Computer Science so this is the first step! I had taken AP Computer Science in high school but taking an intro class again was beyond helpful. The professor was great and I learned so many new things. Also, each Computer Science class has a lab section. In lab we work on our homework and can get help from the Professor, fellow classmates, and a TA. It is really nice to have this because I could get my homework done most of the time.
Monday, Wednesday, Friday 12:40 - 1:40 PM
This is a required thing for all incoming freshman at Willamette. We got to choose Colloquium topics that seemed interesting and they would put us in one of our five choices. I don't think Genres of the Self was on the top of my list, I don't even remember selecting it. I was nervous about how it would turn out. All of my nervousness quickly went away because this class was so much fun. I had a blast. We learned about different ways we form our identities and the readings were all interesting and the Professor was phenomenal. Great class.
